## What You'll Have When Done
A lightning-fast, privacy-first smart home hub running directly on your home server's network adapter (`port 8123`). Home Assistant automatically scans your Wi-Fi network to discover and pair with Apple HomeKit, Sonos, Google Cast, Philips Hue, and Zigbee/Z-Wave devices—allowing you to control everything from one single app even when your internet connection drops!

## Why Choose Home Assistant?

Commercial smart home gadgets routinely rely on cloud servers: when you press a switch on your wall, the signal travels to a data center across the country just to turn on the bulb above your head. Home Assistant executes your automations 100% locally with zero latency and complete privacy.

## Onboarding & Setup Guide

### 1. Launching the Container
Navigate to the `home-automation` stack directory and launch the compose stack:
```bash
cd stacks/home-automation
docker compose up -d
```

### 2. First-Run Wizard
Open `http://YOUR_SERVER_IP:8123` in your web browser. Home Assistant takes about 1–2 minutes on first boot to prepare its internal database and required dependencies. Once ready, follow the wizard to set your name, username, and home location (used for sunrise/sunset triggers).

### 3. Pairing Your First Devices
1. Once logged in, go to **Settings** → **Devices & Services**.
2. Home Assistant automatically displays discovered devices on your network right under the **Discovered** banner!
3. Click **Configure** on any device (such as Apple TV, Sonos, or Philips Hue) to pair it instantly without needing cloud accounts.
